About Me

Engineering Student with a Biomed Focus

I’m a Nanotechnology Engineering 🛠️ student 🎓 at the University of Waterloo with a passion for solving real-world problems at the intersection of science 🔬, engineering 🛠️, and biology 🧬. Through experience in research 🔍 and software development 💻, I enjoy turning complex ideas into practical, efficient solutions.

During my co-ops, I’ve worked across diverse environments. From medical device testing 🧪 on the R&D team at Siemens Healthineers to building a product-recommendation chatbot 🤖 that reached over 10,000 users at a New York–based startup. Along with my current internship in Northern Ireland at Randox Laboratories, I’m currently completing a biosensor 🧬 capstone project focused on developing a home test for melanoma.

Outside the lab, I’ve been a Varsity field hockey 🏑 player while maintaining a full engineering course load, and am proud to have represented Team Ontario for 5+ seasons in my youth. Along with being a lot of fun, the sport has shaped my discipline, teamwork 🤝, and resilience 💪. These are values I carry into my academic and professional work.

My interests span data analytics, CAD design, software, and experimental lab work, and I’m especially drawn to projects that combine technical depth, creative thinking, and biomed. I value teamwork, adaptability, and clear communication, and I’m always looking for opportunities to learn, build, and contribute to meaningful technologies.

In my free time, I enjoy traveling ✈️ and expressing my creativity through painting 🎨, working with oils, acrylics, and large-scale murals.
